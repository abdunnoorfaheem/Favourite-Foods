My favorite food Lists

  • Macher Jhol (Bengali Fish Curry):-

    Bangladesh and especially Kolkatta, being situated on one of the largest delta basins and mangrove coasts in the world, enjoys a bountiful supply of seafood, freshwater, and seawater fish. But there is one fish that is especially close to the heart of every Bengali: the infamous Hilsa or Ilish.

  • Haji Biriyani

    Haji Biryani (also known as Hajir Biryani ) is one of the oldest restaurants in Bangladesh , located in Old Dhaka, serving goat meat biryani , borhani (salty mint drink made with curd) and soft drinks. The restaurant was started in 1939 by a man named Haji Mohammad Hossain as a roadside food stall. The company later became part of the city's culture through dramatic changes in its business

  • Chingri Malai Curry

    Changri malai curry is the most famous Bangladesh curry dish based on coconut milk, and is revered throughout the region, including India. Chingri Malai (or Chingri Macher Malaikari as it is sometimes known), is a delicious creamy, satisfying seafood curry that is prepared by cooking prawns or shrimp in a coconut milk sauce with aromatic spices and served with basmati rice. Coconut milk is a very common ingredient in Bangladeshi cuisine, and this Changri Malai curry is a perfect example of that.
